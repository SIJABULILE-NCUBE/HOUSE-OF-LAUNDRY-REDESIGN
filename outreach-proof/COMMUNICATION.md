## A Little About Me and This Project

My name is Sijabulile Ncube. I am currently studying software development, with a particular focus on front-end web development using React and modern design tools. As part of my coursework, I was asked to choose a real, existing South African business and design a modern redesign concept of their website — the idea being to practise identifying real opportunities for improvement, rather than working from an imaginary brief.

I looked at a number of businesses before choosing yours. What stood out to me about House of Laundry & Dry Cleaners was the sheer range of what you offer — not just household laundry, but dry cleaning, takkie washing, dyeing, alterations, and a genuinely impressive commercial and industrial side serving hotels, hospitals, and businesses across ten different sectors. That is a lot of real value that I felt deserved to be presented properly online.

## Why I Believe a Redesign Could Help

I want to be respectful here — your business is clearly well-established and well-loved, and getting a website online at all is no small achievement. My intention was never to criticise, but simply to look, with fresh eyes, at where a modern refresh could help the site do justice to how well the business itself actually runs. A few examples of what I noticed:

- The site's footer still shows a 2020 copyright date, and there is a leftover COVID-19 banner from a few years ago — small details, but ones that can unintentionally make an active, thriving business look less current than it is.
- Several images on the site do not load properly, which likely is a technical issue rather than anything anyone did wrong, but it does mean visitors sometimes see broken image icons instead of photos of your branches.
- Your per-kilogram pricing, which I think is genuinely one of your best selling points, is a little hard to find on the current site.
- There currently isn't a simple way for a customer to put together a request online — they need to call or message directly, which works, but a website tool could make it even easier for busy customers.

## What I Built, In Detail

**Design and branding**
I designed the concept around a clean, modern, silver-and-charcoal aesthetic with a touch of glass-panel styling, while deliberately keeping your real cyan brand colour and your motto, "We do it with pride," at the centre of the design. I recreated your wave-and-hanger logo shape myself in code, since I did not want to copy the original file without your explicit permission — if you like the direction and would ever want to use your real logo file instead, that would be a simple thing to update.

**Pages and how they work**

- *Home page* — gives visitors an immediate sense of what you offer: your four branches, your seven-day opening hours, and your free delivery radius, right at the top.
- *Services page* — lays out all of your household services (laundry, ironing, dry cleaning, takkie wash, dyeing, alterations) with real prices shown clearly, so customers know what to expect before they even walk in.
- *Commercial page* — presents your industrial and commercial linen services properly, using real photographs of your branches and services that I sourced from your website and your Instagram page, with your permission to do so. It also lists all ten industries you serve, from healthcare to mining.
- *Branches page* — shows all four of your branches with their real addresses. A visitor can tap "Call" to phone that branch directly on their phone, or "Directions" to open Google Maps straight to that location — removing any guesswork about how to find you.
- *Reviews page* — includes a few sample customer reviews with a "like" button, purely to demonstrate how social proof could work if you wanted to build this out further with real customer feedback over time.
- *Order page* — this is the feature I am most excited about. A customer can browse your services, add the items they need cleaned to a basket, see a running total build up, and then send the whole request straight to you with one tap, either via WhatsApp or email. No payment happens on the site itself — it simply compiles a clear, ready-to-send request, so you can then confirm pricing, availability, and collection exactly as you do now. It is designed to save time for both you and the customer, not to change how you actually run things.
- *Contact page and floating WhatsApp button* — available on every page, so getting in touch is never more than one tap away.
- *Light and dark mode* — the whole site can switch between a bright, airy look and a darker, more dramatic look, depending on what a visitor prefers.

**Technology used**
For transparency, since I know this may be of interest: the site is built using React (a modern JavaScript framework), styled with Tailwind CSS, and uses React Router to move between pages without full page reloads, which makes it feel fast and smooth on a phone. It is fully responsive, meaning it adjusts properly whether someone is viewing it on a small phone screen, a tablet, or a desktop computer.
