# Company Project 1 — Brief

## Which company did I choose?

House of Laundry & Dry Cleaners — a laundry and dry cleaning business based in Soweto, Johannesburg, with four branches (Rockville, Dube Village, Tladi, and Mapetla Ext). They offer wash/dry/fold, dry cleaning, takkie (sneaker) wash, clothing dyeing, alterations, and commercial/industrial laundry services. Their website is [houseoflaundry.co.za](https://houseoflaundry.co.za).

## How did I find it?

I was looking specifically for a South African small business that had its own domain (not just a free Wix or Facebook page) but whose website clearly hadn't been maintained properly. I browsed through a few different industries and found House of Laundry through a web search for Soweto-based service businesses with their own `.co.za` domains.

## Why did I choose it?

A few reasons made this one stand out:

- It's a **real, active business** with multiple branches, real phone numbers, a WhatsApp presence, and active social media (Instagram, Facebook) — so the business itself is clearly operating, the website just doesn't reflect that.
- The **range of services is genuinely interesting** to design around — they don't just do laundry, they also handle commercial/industrial linen for hospitals, hotels, restaurants and mining companies, all to SANS 10146 standard. That's a lot of real content that's currently buried or poorly presented.
- The pricing model (per kg, not per item) is something most people wouldn't expect, which gives me something specific to highlight in the redesign rather than just making a generic services page.
- Their brand identity (the cyan/turquoise wave-hanger logo, the "We do it with pride" tagline) is actually quite distinctive — it just isn't being used well on the current site.

## Why does it need a redesign?

The current site has several clear problems:

1. **The footer says "Copyright © 2020"** — it hasn't been updated in six years.
2. **There's still a COVID-19 banner** on the homepage linking to sacoronavirus.co.za — a pandemic-era leftover from 2020 that nobody removed.
3. **Multiple images are literally broken** — the HTML source has empty image tags (`<img src="">`) scattered throughout the gallery and hero slider, so visitors see broken image icons instead of actual photos of the laundromat.
4. **The site runs on an old WordPress theme** with a Slider Revolution plugin that has a broken/empty slide in the hero.
5. **The pricing is buried** — they charge per kilogram (not per item), which is actually a selling point, but you'd have to dig through a separate PDF or page to find the actual prices. Most visitors would never see them.
6. **No online ordering or request form** — despite already offering delivery within 5km of one branch and servicing commercial clients across Joburg, there's no way to request a pickup or submit an order online. You have to call or WhatsApp.
7. **The design says "Designed by Basic Hosting"** in the footer — it was built as a basic template site and never evolved past that.

The business deserves better than what the website is showing. They have four branches, a 7-day operating schedule, commercial certifications, and a genuine range of services — none of that comes through on the current site.
